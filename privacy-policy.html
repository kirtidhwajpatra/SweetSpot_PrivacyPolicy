03

How the App Works

SweetSpot uses Apple's native Network framework and URLSession APIs to measure the quality of your active WiFi or cellular connection. Here is what happens step by step:

  • You open the app and start a session. The app begins sending small, anonymous probe requests to measure how fast your connection can send and receive data.
  • As you walk around your space, the app continuously re-measures download speed, latency (round-trip time), and jitter (variability in latency).
  • These three raw values are combined on-device into a single score from 0 to 100 displayed in real time.
  • When you end the session, the result is saved locally to your device for your own reference.
  • No data is sent anywhere outside your device at any point during or after this process.
Note on network probes: To measure speed, the app makes real network requests. These are anonymous ping and data-transfer probes — they carry no personal identifiers, no device ID, and no user information. They are functionally no different from your browser loading a web page.

Third-Party Services

SweetSpot integrates no third-party SDKs, analytics libraries, advertising networks, or data brokers. The following are explicitly absent from the app's codebase:

  • Google Analytics, Firebase Analytics, or any other Google SDK
  • Facebook (Meta) SDK or Audience Network
  • Amplitude, Mixpanel, Segment, or any product analytics tool

  • In-app advertising networks (AdMob, MoPub, etc.)

The only external network activity is the anonymous speed-probe requests described in Section 3. These requests are stateless and carry no identifiers that could be linked to you.

Future changes: If a future version of SweetSpot introduces optional features that require a server — for example, cloud history sync or cross-device sharing — this policy will be updated and you will be notified inside the app before any change takes effect. All such features will be explicitly opt-in.

Data Storage & Retention

All data generated by SweetSpot lives in your iPhone's secure app sandbox — an isolated container that other apps cannot read and that we cannot access.

Retention periods

  • Session results: Stored until you manually delete them in-app, or until you uninstall SweetSpot.
  • Preferences: Stored until the app is uninstalled.
  • Server-side retention: None — because we receive no data, there is nothing for us to retain, delete, or manage on your behalf.

iCloud Backup

SweetSpot's local data may be included in your device's iCloud backup if you have iCloud Backup enabled in iOS Settings. This is controlled entirely by you and Apple — not by us. To exclude SweetSpot from iCloud backup, go to: Settings → [Your Name] → iCloud → Show All → SweetSpot → toggle Off.

How to delete all your data

Uninstalling SweetSpot from your device permanently removes all locally stored app data. No request to us is necessary because we hold nothing remotely.

Security

Because SweetSpot does not transmit or store personal data remotely, the attack surface is minimal by design. The primary protection for your in-app data is your device's own security:

  • App sandbox data is protected by iOS's built-in file-level encryption when your device is locked (Data Protection class: Protected Until First User Authentication).
  • Anonymous network probes are sent over HTTPS using TLS 1.2 or higher and carry no personal identifiers.
  • The app requests no unnecessary iOS permissions — no camera, microphone, contacts, photos, or location access.

We recommend keeping your device updated to the latest iOS version and using Face ID or a strong alphanumeric passcode to protect your device and its contents.

Responsible disclosure: If you discover a security vulnerability in SweetSpot, please email us directly (see Section 10) before making it public. We will acknowledge your report within 72 hours and aim to resolve confirmed issues within 14 days.
